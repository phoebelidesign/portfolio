A short film presenting a deliberately tedious experience with no clear focus or urgency. A person's senses are constantly collecting data; to avoid sensory overload, the brain filters this flood of stimuli, prioritising what seems most relevant for survival and discarding the rest. But what happens to the signals deemed unimportant? Loiter Here explores the state of autopilot, a liminal space of existing within ambient noise. What counts as meaningful? Distinguishes one cue from another? Viewers are invited to reflect on their meaning-making process. Exhibited at MPavillion Parkville for Melbourne Design Week 2025.
