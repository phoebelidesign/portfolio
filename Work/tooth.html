Typography

A modular typeface constructed using right angled isosceles triangles as a base unit, creating structures that feel both jagged and fluid. An inquiry into the relationship between blackletter and glyphic styles. The blackletter style inspires the dense and angular quality of Tooth's letterforms, with a less ornamental and more geometric approach. Though Tooth is formally a sans serif typeface, the triangular modules create an illusion of serifs, echoing the sharpness of glyphic letterforms.
